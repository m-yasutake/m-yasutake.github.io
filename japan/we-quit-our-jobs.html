We Quit Our Jobs and We're Going to Japan

T
Mika & Tom
March 4, 2026 5 min read
← Back to Japan

Here's the short version: we handed in our notices, spent three frantic months building our bikes, buying gear, and trying not to think too hard about what we were doing — and tomorrow morning we fly to Tokyo.

The longer version is messier and more honest. We'd been talking about doing something big for years. A proper trip. Not a two-week holiday with a return flight already booked, but a real, open-ended, see-what-happens adventure. The kind where you don't know exactly what the end will look like when you're standing at the beginning.

Japan came up because of Mika's family connections — and because the idea of cycling from one end of Japan to the other, from the southern tip of Kyushu all the way north through Honshu and up into Hokkaido, felt like exactly the scale of thing we were dreaming of. Then Tom said "and we could do Norway after" and somehow that stuck too. His mum's family is from Egersund — a tiny harbour town on the southwest coast — and cycling from there to Tromsø, up past the Arctic Circle, felt equal parts beautiful and completely mad. So obviously we said yes to both.

The bikes took longer than expected. Of course they did. Tom's rear derailleur situation was a saga that involved three different mechanics and a lot of YouTube tutorials. Mika's front rack broke during a test ride three weeks ago and had to be re-welded. We did a fully loaded overnight test in Wales last month and came back drenched, exhausted, and completely convinced this was a good idea.

The weeks leading up to this were a blur of bike prep, packing lists, and the surprisingly emotional process of putting our lives into storage. Everything that didn't fit into four panniers and a handlebar bag went into boxes. There was a brief moment of irony when we spotted the moving company's name on the biggest box in our storage unit: Håkull — based in Stavanger, just down the coast from Egersund. Even our cardboard boxes are pointing us toward Norway.

Right now, as I type this, there are two fully loaded touring bikes standing in the hallway. We have four panniers, a handlebar bag, one drybag each strapped to the rear rack, a small tent, two sleeping bags, and approximately one too many pairs of cycling socks. We've booked our flights and a hotel in Tokyo for the first week — we're visiting friends and family there before flying down to Kagoshima to start the actual bike journey. After that? We'll figure it out as we go.

We don't have a fixed itinerary. We don't have every night planned. What we do have is each other, working bikes, and an extremely optimistic attitude about our ability to read a Japanese map.

Tomorrow it starts. We leave at 6am. Neither of us has slept properly in a week.

— Mika & Tom, the night before
